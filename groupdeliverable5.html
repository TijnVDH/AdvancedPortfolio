PROOF OF CONCEPTS

DELIVERABLE CONTAINING MY WORK IN PROOF OF CONCEPTS

INTRO

For the final stages of the group project, proof of concepts was something me and the group longed for. While the final deliverable our stakeholder Dennis wished for was a prototype, I thought it was a good idea to work on these proof of concepts together with some group members.

REQUIREMENTS

When discussing with the group, a list of three proof of concepts came forward that we would work on.

  • A live decibel meter with visualisation.
  • The daily dose screen filling up based on the decibel meter values from the first item.

These proof of concepts would be used on the day of Innovation Insights, and displayed on big screens during the presentations.

APPROACH

By making use of the proof of concept method from the CMD Methods, me and a group member started working in HTML, CSS and JavaScript on the first proof of concept.

The approach was that I started working on the visualisation, while the other group members worked on the decibel meter. Eventually I would make a merge request to combine the work of us both.

By following a YouTube tutorial, I quickly got the hang of the Web Audio API. I ended up with a visualisation that could be merged with the live decibel meter.

END RESULT

After having succesfully merged code in Gitlab, the proof of concept was ready to be used. The proof of concept can be accessed through here.
