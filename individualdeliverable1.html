USER RESEARCH PLAN

REPORT CONTAINING THE RESEARCH PLAN WHICH HAS BEEN CONDUCTED WITH USERS.

INTRO

This deliverable page presents a summary of the user research plan which has been conducted with users of the portfolio. The page gives a summary of the process which has been taken, from setting up goals to execution. It is suggested to read through this summary first for a better understanding, after which to use the button at the bottom of the page to refer to as the actual proof document.

GOALS

To clarify what I intend to achieve with this research plan, I set up my goals as SMART as possible:

  • Ik wil begrijpen welke stappen een gebruiker doorloopt om een bepaalde taak in het portfolio te voltooien m.b.v. diverse onderzoeksmethodes, waar ik tijdens de uitvoering achter ga komen in een tijdsspan van ca. één uur.
  • Ik wil ontdekken wat de beredenering van de feedback van een gebruiker is, dus waarom de gebruiker vindt wat hij/zij vindt, a.d.h.v. het toepassen van diverse onderzoeksmethodes en tools, tijdens het interview met de gebruiker.
  • Ik ga pijn- en verbeterpunten opstellen a.d.h.v. de resultaten die ik van gebruikers heb gekregen tijdens het interview met diverse tools, om erachter te komen wat er echt anders moet in mijn portfolio, wat na het uitvoeren van het onderzoeksplan gebeurt.

TARGET GROUP

The target group for this project are mainly teachers/assessors. Since the portfolio acts as the big piece of evidence of what I have done this semester, assessors need to be able to access my work easily with this portfolio. METHODOLOGY

After knowing who I would need to gather insights, I had to create a plan of approach for the methodology. I often use Nielsen Norman Group to pick suitable research methods. This is mainly because they offer thorough explanation on executing these research methods. Since I knew what my goals were, I quickly got to some suitable research methods. The portfolio is not that big, so two to three methods would suffice to gain insights on all aspects of the portfolio. The research methods are conducted one on one with the user:

  • Short user interview (to clarify the target group)
  • Contextual inquiry
  • Task-based usability test

NOTABLE RESULTS

After conducting this research plan, several results came forward. In general, the structure of the portfolio is clear and easy to navigate through. However, some visual elements were mentioned several times, such as the text being too big, or unnecessary elements grabbing the attention.

The information the users receive on screens are fine. To solutions to these pain points are several design changes, which means that there is nothing major that needs to be changed. This was something that I had expected, which can be seen in the hypotheses of the goals inside the document.
