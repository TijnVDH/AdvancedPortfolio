INDIVIDUAL PROJECT

USER RESEARCH PLAN

REPORT CONTAINING THE RESEARCH PLAN WHICH HAS BEEN CONDUCTED WITH USERS.

INTRO

This deliverable page presents a summary of the user research plan which has been conducted with users of the portfolio. The page gives a summary of the process which has been taken, from setting up goals to execution. It is suggested to read through this summary first for a better understanding, after which to use the button at the bottom of the page to refer to as the actual proof document. The linked research question to this deliverable is:

  • Hoe kan ik een gebruiksvriendelijke portfolio opstellen, waardoor de gebruiker niet zelf naar informatie hoeft te zoeken?

GOALS

To clarify what I intend to achieve with this research plan, I set up my goals as SMART as possible:

  • Ik wil begrijpen welke stappen een gebruiker doorloopt om een bepaalde taak in het portfolio te voltooien m.b.v. diverse onderzoeksmethodes, waar ik tijdens de uitvoering achter ga komen in een tijdsspan van ca. één uur.
  • Ik wil ontdekken wat de beredenering van de feedback van een gebruiker is, dus waarom de gebruiker vindt wat hij/zij vindt, a.d.h.v. het toepassen van diverse onderzoeksmethodes en tools, tijdens het interview met de gebruiker.
  • Ik ga pijn- en verbeterpunten opstellen a.d.h.v. de resultaten die ik van gebruikers heb gekregen tijdens het interview met diverse tools, om erachter te komen wat er echt anders moet in mijn portfolio, wat na het uitvoeren van het onderzoeksplan gebeurt.

TARGET GROUP

The target group for this project are mainly teachers/assessors. Since the portfolio acts as the big piece of evidence of what I have done this semester, assessors need to be able to access my work easily with this portfolio. This is of course the intended target group, but the target group has been confirmed with the help of the execution of this research plan.

METHODOLOGY

After knowing who I would need to gather insights, I had to create a plan of approach for the methodology. I often use Nielsen Norman Group to pick suitable research methods. This is mainly because they offer thorough explanation on executing these research methods. Since I knew what my goals were, I quickly got to some suitable research methods. The portfolio is relatively small, so two to three methods would suffice to gain insights on all aspects of the portfolio. The research methods are conducted one on one with the user:

  • Short user interview (to clarify the target group)
  • Contextual inquiry
  • Task-based usability test

NOTABLE RESULTS

After conducting this research plan, several results came forward. In general, the structure of the portfolio is clear and easy to navigate through. However, some visual elements were mentioned several times, such as the text being too big, or unnecessary elements grabbing the attention.

The information the users receive on screens are fine. The solutions to these pain points are several design changes, which means that there is nothing major that needs to be changed. This was something that I had expected, which can be seen in the hypotheses of the goals inside the document.

EVALUATION & NEXT STEPS

After having conducted my research plan with several users, I noticed that the task-based usability mehod was unnecessary. While making use of the contextual inquiry, all of the users who tested the portfolio already fulfilled the tasks I was planning to give during the task-based usability test afterwards. This unfortunately meant that I had accounted for extra time which was not needed.

Referring back to the research question I initially planned to answer, I have received feedback from all of the users to iterate on my portfolio to create an end portfolio which is user-friendly.

After conducting my research plan with several users, I analyzed the gathered results. These results will help me to iterate on my designs. These are mainly small design choice changes, but when I will start developing I will still have update meetings. During these meetings, I aim to receive advice on how to achieve the final goal I am aiming for.

The validated design for my portfolio will then be used for the start of the development. By having created the designated style and structure in these wireframes and mockups, the start of the development process is straightforward. The result of this deliverable caused for the following action list:

  • Make use of only necessary research methods for the user research plan in the group project.
  • Create iterations on the design based on the results of the user research plan.
  • Start with development of portfolio with the help of previously created portfolio.
  • Keep asking for feedback on additional features when necessary.
