INDIVIDUAL PROJECT

PORTFOLIO ITERATIONS

DELIVERABLE CONTAINING ITERATIONS THAT HAVE BEEN MADE DURING THE COURSE OF DEVELOPMENT OF THE PORTFOLIO.

INTRO

After having finished conducting my user research plan, and started development on my portfolio, I still was not entirely satisfied with the look of my portfolio. The related research question to this deliverable is:

  • Hoe kan ik een gebruiksvriendelijke portfolio opstellen, waardoor de gebruiker niet zelf naar informatie hoeft te zoeken?

ITERATION 1

After starting development of my portfolio, I have made several iterations on the aesthetic and functionality of the portfolio. Unfortunately, these changes had not been validated with actual users, but by making use of the ten usability heuristics (10 Usability Heuristics for User Interface Design, n.d.), I was able to conclude that these changes add more depth to the portfolio.

The first iteration that had been made is a triangular background on the homepage. This background looks as follows:

The triangular pattern was chosen because because they are versatile and can create a variety of looks. They can be used to add a sense of dimension to a room, make a statement, or simply add a touch of elegance (Wallpaper Kenya, 2024).

ITERATION 2

The second iteration that was made, was by adding more buttons on the landing screen. These additional buttons were for accessing the projects and the proof table. The button for the proof table was attached to the navbar, for ease of access to the proof of the portfolio, since this was preferred by my semester coach.
